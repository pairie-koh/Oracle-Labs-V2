# Historian Methodology Change — 2026-03-16

**Version:** 1.2.0 → 1.3.0
**Change:** Reduced `REVERSION_RATE` from 0.03 to 0.01

## Reasoning

Historian MSE (0.00029491) is still 3.7x the naive baseline (0.00008054). The most recent cycle's MSE (0.00051388) regressed from the prior cycle's low (0.00021677), suggesting 1.2.0's MAX_NEWS_SHIFT reduction did not lock in an improvement. The fundamental problem is that any deviation from the current price adds error, since prices move only modestly over 4-hour windows.

The 2026-03-15 log showed the news signal was the dominant variance source, but that was already addressed by halving MAX_NEWS_SHIFT in 1.2.0. The next largest systematic contributor is `REVERSION_RATE`, which pulls the forecast away from the current price every cycle regardless of market conditions. At 0.03, this creates a consistent drag toward the 7-day median even when the market is trending away from it. Reducing to 0.01 makes the historian more conservative — closer to the naive baseline — while preserving the directional framework and the news signal. If prices are trending, fighting the trend with a 3% reversion pull adds error without adding value.
