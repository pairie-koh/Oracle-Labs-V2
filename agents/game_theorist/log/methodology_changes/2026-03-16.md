# Methodology Change — 2026-03-16

**Version:** 1.2.0 → 1.3.0
**Change:** `bias_adjustment` now only applied to directional facts (`direction != 0.0`); unclassified facts contribute nothing to `net_signal`

## Reasoning

Yesterday's sensitivity reduction (0.015 → 0.001) significantly improved the last-cycle MSE from ~0.05 to 0.00592, confirming the directional logic is sound — only the magnitude was wrong. However, overall MSE remains ~0.039 due to persistent upward noise: `bias_adjustment = actor["escalation_bias"] * signal_weight * 0.5` was being added to every fact regardless of whether its category was escalatory, de-escalatory, or unclassified. Since most actor profiles carry positive escalation_bias, facts that fall outside the defined category lists (direction=0) were silently accumulating upward pressure on net_signal each cycle. By zeroing out bias_adjustment when direction=0, unclassified facts become true no-ops, eliminating this structural noise source and making the model's output depend only on categorically meaningful signals.
