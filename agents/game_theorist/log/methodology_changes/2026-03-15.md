# Methodology Change — 2026-03-15

**Version:** 1.1.0 → 1.2.0
**Change:** Reduced `ESCALATION_SENSITIVITY` from `0.015` to `0.001`

## Reasoning

The agent had MSE of 0.04109 — 514× worse than the naive baseline — despite 97% per-market directional accuracy. This combination means the predictions were correct in direction but catastrophically large in magnitude. With net signal values typically in the 3–10 range, the old sensitivity produced 4-hour forecast shifts of 5–15 percentage points, while the market rarely moves more than 0.5pp in that window. Dropping sensitivity 15× (0.015 → 0.001) brings predicted shifts to sub-1pp territory, consistent with a market where the naive baseline barely budges. The directional logic (costly signals, consensus scaling, actor credibility) is sound and preserved; only the magnitude scaling was wrong.
