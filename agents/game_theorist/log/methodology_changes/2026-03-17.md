# Methodology Change — 2026-03-17

**Version:** 1.3.0 → 1.4.0

**Change:** Removed `bias_adjustment` from the signal contribution formula.

**What changed:** Previously, every directional fact included a `bias_adjustment = actor["escalation_bias"] * signal_weight * 0.5` additive term. Since most source categories have positive escalation_bias (government_official=0.15, osint=0.2, social_media=0.3), this systematically inflated the net signal in the escalatory direction regardless of what the facts actually said. The contribution is now simply `score × direction`.

**Why:** The agent had 0% directional accuracy over 6 predictions and MSE 5.9× worse than the naive baseline — a clear sign of systematic bias. The costly-signal mechanism already captures the information in `escalation_bias` (a de-escalatory signal from an escalation-biased source is weighted more). The `bias_adjustment` was double-counting actor bias and introducing a persistent upward drift.

**Expected effect:** Reduced systematic escalatory bias; net signal driven purely by credibility × confidence × direction. Should improve directional accuracy on cycles where de-escalatory facts dominate.
